FIRST Robotics Competition

The Competition

FRC isn't just about robots. It's about combining the excitement of sport with the rigors of science and technology. We call it "The Sport for the Mind."

What is FRC?

Under strict rules, limited resources, and an intense six-week time limit, teams of students are challenged to raise funds, design a team "brand," hone teamwork skills, and build and program industrial-size robots to play a difficult field game against like-minded competitors.

Itâ€™s as close to "real-world engineering" as a student can get. Volunteer professional mentors lend their time and talents to guide each team.

  • Alliance-based 3v3 Gameplay

Season at a Glance

Kickoff

The global game reveal! We receive the game manual and immediately start brainstorming strategy and design.

2

Build Season

The grind begins. We CAD, fabricate, wire, and program the robot. Long nights and hard work define this period.

3

Mar - Apr

Districts

We travel across Texas to compete in district events, aiming to earn enough points to qualify for States.

4

Late April

Championship

The goal: The FIRST World Championship in Houston. The best teams from around the globe compete for the title.
